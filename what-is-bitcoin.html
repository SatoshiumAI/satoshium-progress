The simple definition

Bitcoin is digital money with rules, not rulers.

It is a global peer-to-peer network that lets people send value directly, without needing a bank, payment company, or government to approve the transaction. Bitcoin is open, verifiable, and enforced by math and consensus.

How it works (plain English)

Bitcoin is a public ledger secured by proof-of-work.

1) The blockchain

Think of the blockchain as a public notebook where transactions are recorded in ‚Äúblocks.‚Äù Each new block is linked to the previous one, making history extremely hard to rewrite.

2) Keys and ownership

Wallets hold cryptographic keys. Your private key proves you can spend your bitcoin. Your public address is where others can send bitcoin to you.

3) Mining and security

Miners compete to add the next block by expending real-world energy (proof-of-work). This makes attacks expensive and aligns security with physical reality.

4) Nodes and verification

Anyone can run a Bitcoin node to independently verify the rules and the ledger. Nodes are how the network resists manipulation: don‚Äôt trust ‚Äî verify.
